I am new to coding, i have dabbled in learning to code for a number of years, but never really took it seriously. I the spring of 2000 i went to NorthEastern University for a certificate degree. Courses were Visual Basic, Advanced Visual Basic, C++, HTML, Perl, along with Access database, and a few other courses of which i cannot remember them. Completed the degree in summer of 2001, but never continued from there. Instead went to Wentworth Institute of Technology for a bachelors degree in Electronics Engineering.

Ever since i was little i have always had a knack for building/repairing things, each time i got a radio or some other electronics device i would eventually take it apart to see what was in side, and try to build something else from it, but i was also intersted in science as well ( physics, chemistry, astronomy, geology ), i would spends hours at a time reading books etc... many trips to and from the town library each week. As i got older i began to dabble in computers, started buying the components and building systems rather thn buying new ones, eventually i would sell the previous one then build another one, this went on for years. until 4 years ago when i gave up on desktop systems and went with laptops.

In 1999 i left the restaurant business which i had been in for almost 12 years, and persued a career in field service working as an independant agent for Xerox repairing copiers, held that position until the end of 2002, afterwhich i took a field tech position for a small family run company in Brighton, MA called Just The Fax. After almost 2 years, i left and went to work for another small father/son run company called Town Business Systems in Norwood,MA, both jobs were repairing small printers, faxes and small size copiers. Then in December 2005 interviewed for a field tech position for a company based in Dallas Texas called Compucom Systemsa very large company operating in US and many other countries, i have been working with them for almost 10 years. I have determined it is time now to break off from field tech work and begin a new career path, a path in Web developement.
